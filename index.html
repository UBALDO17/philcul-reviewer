Philippine Tourist Destinations
Major Attractions per Region · as of Nov 2022
Core Concepts
Attraction
The main reason people visit a destination. A place of interest due to its inherent natural beauty, historical, or cultural significance. Values: historical importance, cultural value, political significance, nature, built beauty, leisure & amusement.
Destination (UNWTO)
"The place visited that is central to the decision to take the trip." (Bierman) "A country, state, region, city, or town which is marketed as a place for tourists to visit." A destination often has more than one attraction.
Key Difference
A destination is an area with some attractions that earns money from tourism, whereas an attraction is a specific place that attracts tourists. Example: Manila (destination) → Rizal Park & Intramuros (attractions).

Luzon
NCR National Capital Region ▾
Manila Capital City
PH's capital — top shopping, arts, cultural immersion, rest & recreation. Near lakeshore towns of Laguna de Bai (arts & crafts).
  • Intramuros Heritage
    Oldest & most historic place in Manila. Name means "within the walls." Encircled by high stone walls with castle-like earthworks. Contains:
    • San Agustin Church — UNESCO World Heritage Site; oldest church in the Philippines
    • Fort Santiago — one of the oldest fortifications in Manila, built by the Spaniards in 1571; now a memorial to WWII victims
  • Rizal Park
    Premiere people's park with world-class sculptures, historical markers, beautiful gardens, photo & art exhibit facilities. Flagship of freedom and courage — venue for political rallies, oath-takings, and national events.
  • National Museum of the Philippines
    Government agency museum with research and conservation programs nationwide. Houses:
    • National Museum of Fine Arts
    • National Museum of Anthropology
    • National Museum of Natural History
    • National Planetarium
  • Manila Ocean Park Marine Theme Park
    Country's first world-class marine theme park. Integrated urban resort with aquatic life attractions and aqua-themed hotel. Features the Oceanarium (walkthrough of marine creatures indigenous to PH & SE Asia), Sea Lion Show, Star Bird Show.
Quezon City
Populous city focused on cultural, historical, and educational tourism.
  • Quezon Memorial Circle
    27-hectare national park dedicated to the memory of President Manuel Luis Quezon. Monument at the center has a museum with Quezon's memorabilia and his remains inside the shrine.
  • Art in Island Museum
    3D art museum founded by Yun Jae Kyoung and 18 Korean master painters. Art gives illusion of depth when viewed from a certain angle — designed as backdrops for photos.
📍 Also in NCR: Mall of Asia (Pasay) · The Mind Museum (Taguig) · Bamboo Organ & St. Joseph Parish Church (Las Piñas) · Ayala Museum (Makati)
CAR Cordillera Administrative Region ▾
Benguet Salad Bowl of PH
Main source of fresh vegetables. Picturesque mountain views, cool atmosphere. Baguio City = Summer Capital of the PH (avg. 15–23°C) & City of Pines. Panagbenga Festival = annual flower festival every February (Kankanaey word = "season of blooming").
  • Baguio Cathedral
    Familiar Baguio landmark with a distinct pink facade, rose windows, and twin square belfry — completed in 1936.
  • Burnham Park
    City's premier and oldest park in Baguio. Gardens and a lake at the city center. Activities: biking, skating, boarding, boating.
  • Wright Park
    Popular for horseback riding and souvenir shopping. Long stone stairway leads to the "Pool of Pines" — 100-meter-long pool lined with Baguio pine trees. Opposite the main gate of The Mansion (official summer residence of the President of the Philippines; has a mini museum of former presidents' memorabilia).
  • Colors of Stobosa
    Hillside home artwork in La Trinidad, Benguet. Magic colors transformed stone-hill houses into an artwork symbolizing "re-blooming" and "re-greening" of the environment. A global transformation icon.
📍 Also in Benguet: Mines View Park · Mt. Pulag
Mountain Province
Known for hanging coffins where mummified ancestors are found. Historical sites include the Dap-ay / Ato (where tribal elders decide on issues), Lang-ay Cultural Festival.
  • Sugong Hanging Coffins (Sugong Cave)
    Located in Echo Valley, Sagada. Locals place their deceased in coffins and transport them to a cave for burial — a unique burial tradition.
  • Sumaguing Cave
    One of the Philippines' most famous spelunking sites.
  • Bontoc Museum
    Features artifacts for domestic and traditional purposes. Beside the museum is a model of a traditional Bontoc village highlighting Bontoc material culture.
Ifugao
Bulul = standing male figure representing a rice deity; an essential expression of anthropomorphic sculpture in northern PH. Gotad Ad Ifugao Festival — held every June to showcase old culture and traditions.
  • Banaue Rice Terraces UNESCO
    Dubbed "Eighth Wonder of the World." UNESCO World Heritage Site. Cascading rice terraces carved into mountainsides 2,000 years ago by Ifugao ancestors. Spread over the Cordillera mountain range.
  • Philippine War Memorial Shrine
    Gigantic pyramid-type concrete structure resembling an oversized Ifugao native house in Linda, Kiangan. Commemorates WWII's end. Depicts Filipino opposition to the Death March and the role of Ifugao and American troops in defeating General Yamashita.
Kalinga
  • Apo Whang-Od (Buscalan Village) Living Treasure
    Home of the last and oldest mambababatok (traditional Kalinga tattoo artist). For over 70 years, Apo Whang-Od has been tattooing Kalinga warriors and women during rites of passage (childbirth, adulthood, marriage, eldership, ancestorship). Tools: soot + water pigment, calamansi thorn on bamboo stick, tapper stick, dried bamboo grass as template.
    • 📍 Also in CAR: Kapakan Falls (Abra) · Lussok Crystal Cave (Apayao)
    Region I Ilocos Region ▾
    Ilocos Norte Capital: Laoag
    Laoag = northernmost urban hub of the Philippines. Has Laoag International Airport. Rich Spanish-era heritage same as Vigan.
    • Paoay Church (Saint Augustine Church) UNESCO
      UNESCO World Heritage Site. One of the Baroque churches and Spanish colonial buildings in Ilocos Norte.
    • Malacañang of the North
      National park and museum in Paoay. Originally the official residence of President Ferdinand Marcos Sr. when visiting his home province.
    • La Paz Sand Dunes
      Sandy coastal desert and beach near Laoag City. Activities: sand surfing and ATV riding through the dunes.
    Ilocos Sur Capital: Vigan
    Vigan City = most well-preserved Spanish colonial town in the Philippines. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in the 16th century. Boasts colonial-era houses, horse-drawn carriages, and Baroque buildings. Even big brands (7-Eleven, McDonald's) adopt traditional colonial building designs to blend in.
    • Calle Crisologo
      Famous cobblestone street lined with Spanish houses. Tourists walk along horse-drawn carriages and rustic mansions showcasing Vigan's rich culture and history.
    • Bantay Bell Tower
      Iconic symbol of Vigan. Built in 1591 as a watchtower. Offers a great view of surrounding towns.
    • Padre Burgos House (NM Ilocos Regional Museum)
      One of three historical buildings of the NM Ilocos Regional Museum Complex. Ancestral house constructed in 1788. Declared historical due to its association with Fr. Jose Burgos, whose death (along with 2 other priests) sparked the anti-Spanish colonial resistance.
    📍 Also in Region I: Bangui Windmills (Bangui, Ilocos Norte) · Hundred Islands National Park (Alaminos, Pangasinan) · Beaches (San Juan, La Union) · Baluarte Watch Tower (Luna, La Union)
    Region II Cagayan Valley ▾
    Batanes Northernmost & Smallest Province
    Indigenous people = Ivatans.
    • Ruins of Songsong
      Ghost barangay in Uyugan — cluster of abandoned old houses with roofless shells after a tidal wave hit Batan island in the 1950s.
    • Honesty Coffee Shop
      Unmanned refreshment store famous worldwide for showcasing Ivatan honesty. Buyers get goods and leave payment in a wooden box — completely on the honor system.
    • Sabtang Island
      One of the most beautiful islands in Batanes. Intermittent sand beaches, steep mountains, and deep canyons.
    Cagayan
    • Callao Cave
      Seven-chambered cave in Peñablanca. Has massive limestones, 7 skylights, and a chapel. Also the home to the oldest human remains found in the Philippines.
    • Cagayan River (Rio Grande de Cagayan)
      Longest and widest river in the Philippines — PH's mightiest watercourse.
    • Palaui Island
      7,000-hectare protected island and serene ecotourism sanctuary. Virgin ecosystems, geological formations, rich terrestrial and aquatic wildlife.
    📍 Also in Region II: Sea of Clouds (Binuangan, Nueva Vizcaya) · Lower Magat Eco-Tourism Park · Quirino Watersports Complex · Governor's Rapids · Aglipay Caves & Campsite (Quirino) · Aguinaldo Shrine & Giant Butaka (Isabela)
    Region III Central Luzon ▾
    Aurora Capital: Baler
    Blessed with abundant tourist attractions — historical sites, waterfalls, lush mountains, panoramic beaches.
    • Museo de Baler
      Features well-preserved artifacts (old jars, bells, porcelains, paintings) and the history of Aurora.
    • Quezon Memorial Park (Baler)
      Birthplace of President Manuel L. Quezon. Marker at the center bears a short-life account of the late President. Original house was made of wood, bamboo, and nipa.
    • Ermita Hill
      2-hectare hill overlooking the Pacific Ocean. During Tromba Marina (Dec 27, 1735), a destructive tidal wave devastated the town — 7 families survived by climbing Ermita Hill. Sculptures depict their story.
    Bataan History Hub
    • Las Casas Filipinas De Acuzar
      Beach resort, convention center, and heritage destination in one. Restored heritage and archival houses allow visitors to feel like they're living in the past.
    • Mt. Samat Shrine (Dambana ng Kagitingan)
      Memorializes the bravery of Filipino and American forces against the Japanese. Approximately 72 hectares from foot to cross. Features a Colonnade (row of tall columns) and a huge Memorial Cross.
    📍 Also in Region III: Mt. Arayat & Mt. Pinatubo (Pampanga) · Anawangin Cove, Nagsasa Cove, Potipot Island, Capones Island (Zambales) · Philippine International Hot-air Balloon Festival (Pampanga)
    Region IV-A CALABARZON ▾
    Batangas
    One of the most popular holiday destinations for domestic travelers due to proximity to Manila.
    • Taal Volcano
      Smallest active volcano in the Philippines. Visitors ride a boat to the foot, then trek or ride a horse to the top.
    • Fortune Island
      Famous for crumbling pillars called "Acropolis" (inspired by the Acropolis of Athens). Once a private luxury island resort — now abandoned due to lack of fresh water and typhoon exposure.
    • Anilao
      Popular diving sites brimming with marine life.
    Laguna
    Endowed with mineral hot & cold springs, waterfalls, and protected forests with diverse flora and fauna.
    • Pagsanjan Falls
      Skilled boatmen take visitors on "shooting the rapids" — maneuvering through turbulent waters at Magdapio River to reach the falls.
    • Enchanted Kingdom
      First and only world-class theme park in the Philippines. Unique rides and attractions in size and magnitude.
    • Pansol Hot Springs
      Hot spring resorts warmed by Mt. Makiling (a dormant volcano).
    Rizal Cradle of Philippine Arts
    • Angono–Binangonan Petroglyphs Oldest Filipino Art
      Discovered by National Artist Carlos "Botong" Francisco in 1965. Most ancient Filipino work of art — dates back to the Stone Age. Famous for 127 rock engravings of human and animal figures.
    • Daranak Falls
      Nestled at the foot of Tanay mountain range. Natural vegetation, forest trees, flowering wild plants, cascading waterfalls, streams, and ditches.
    • Higantes Festival (Angono)
      Secular celebration to honor patron Saint Clement. Parade of giant papier-mâché figures held on November 22 and 23.
    • Mt. Pamitinan (Rodriguez, Rizal)
      Nestled in Sierra Madre Mountain ranges. Spectacular summit view with limestone crags and marble boulders providing various difficulty levels.
    📍 Also in CALABARZON: Beaches & Islands (Quezon) · People's Park in the Sky & Sky Ranch (Tagaytay, Cavite) · Tanay Sea of Clouds (Rizal)
    Region IV-B MIMAROPA ▾
    Palawan Largest Province in PH
    Roughly 1,780 islands and islets including El Nido and Coron. Gorgeous limestone cliffs, white sandy beaches, scuba diving sites, coral reefs, and lush virgin forests. Houses 2 UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
    • Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park UNESCO
      One of the world's most impressive cave systems. Spectacular limestone karst landscapes, old-growth forests, pristine beauty. The underground river flows directly into the sea (lower portion is tidal). Important for biodiversity conservation.
    • Tubbataha Reef Marine Park UNESCO
      Covers 96,828 hectares at the center of the Sulu Sea — includes North & South Atolls and Jessie Beazley Reef. Outstanding isle reef with high concentration of aquatic species. North Islet = nesting site for marine turtles and birds. Features a magnificent 100-meter perpendicular wall, vast lagoons, and 2 coral islands.
    • Calauit Safari Park
      3,700-hectare game reserve and wildlife reserve on Calauit Island in Coron. Contains critically endangered species native to Palawan's Calamian island chain. Safari tours offered.
    • Coron Reefs
      Located on Busuanga Island. Comprises 7 crystal clear, blue lakes enclosed by towering limestone rock formations. Excellent coral reefs teeming with marine life.
    📍 Also in MIMAROPA: Poctoy White Beach (Marinduque) · Tabon Caves (Palawan) · Looc Fish Sanctuary (Romblon) · Puerto Galera (Mindoro)
    Region V Bicol Region (Bicolandia) ▾
    Albay Capital: Legazpi
    • Mayon Volcano National Park Most Active Volcano
      Most active volcano in the Philippines. Known for its perfect cone shape. Home to diverse flora and fauna, including threatened and critically endangered species. Mayon ATV tours offered to tourists.
    • Cagsawa Ruins
      Remains of the 16th-century Franciscan stone church destroyed by Mayon's eruption in 1814. Only the belfry and parts of the convent survived. Now a popular tourist park.
    📍 Also in Region V: Shrine of the Black Nazarene & Calaguas Islands (Camarines Norte) · Peñafrancia Basilica & Caramoan Islands (Camarines Sur) · Donsol Whale Shark Interaction Center (Sorsogon)
    Visayas
    Region VI Western Visayas ▾
    Aklan Oldest Province in PH
    Boracay = Beach Capital of the Philippines. White sand beaches, vibrant community, stunning sunsets. Ranked 1st in Condé Nast Traveler's Top Island: Readers' Choice Awards 2022 in Asia.
    • Willy's Rock (Boracay)
      Popular attraction in Boracay — a statue of the Virgin Mary at the top of a man-made staircase on a rock formation.
    • Boracay Oceanarium
      Has the longest underwater tunnel in the Philippines. Also houses one of the world's largest pearls. Popular attraction for kids.
    • Island Hopping (Boracay)
      Accessible by boat tour: Crocodile Island, Crystal Cove, Magic Island — the three closest islands to Boracay.
    • Ati-Atihan Festival (Kalibo)
      Mardi Gras-style festival held every January. One of the most popular festivals in the country.
    📍 Also in Region VI: Miagao Church (Iloilo) · Alubihod Beaches (Guimaras)
    NIR Negros Island Region ▾
    Negros Occidental Capital: Bacolod
    Bacolod = "Land of Sweet People" (culinary heritage with sweets & desserts) and "City of Smiles" — because of the MassKara Festival (MassKara = "many faces") — a local Mardi Gras combining dance, color, and music.
    • Negros Museum
      Showcases the city's history and records of founding families' culture in the Negros region.
    • The Ruins "Taj Mahal of Negros"
      Remnant structure of Don Mariano Ledesma Lacson's (sugar baron) mansion, burned down during WWII.
    • San Sebastian Cathedral (Bacolod)
      Small chapel that became a cathedral in 1933. Structure made of coral stones from Guimaras Island, completed in 1882 by Fr. Mauricio Ferrero.
    Negros Oriental Capital: Dumaguete
    Dumaguete = "City of Gentle People" — slow-paced lifestyle.
    • Dumaguete Cathedral
      Island's oldest church. Completed in 1776. Local Roman Catholic Diocese seat.
    • Apo Island
      One of the best diving spots in PH. Tourists can snorkel and swim with pawikans (sea turtles) and explore extensive marine biodiversity.
    Siquijor Island of Fire
    Southernmost island in the Visayan group. Spanish name: Isla de Fuegos (Island of Fire) — Spaniards saw twinkling lights at night (actually fireflies surrounding molave trees).
    • Cambugahay Falls
      3-tiered waterfall and swimming hole with clear waters in Lazi municipality.
    • Cantabon Cave
      Famous cave in Siquijor with a long and winding network and beautiful stalactites.
    • Tulapos Marine Sanctuary
      Abundant species of fish, corals, and other underwater marine life.
    📍 Also in NIR: Organic Farm (Negros Occidental) · Casaroro Falls (Negros Oriental) · Lazi Church (Siquijor)
    Region VII Central Visayas ▾
    Bohol
    Home of the famous Chocolate Hills. Local culture expressed in music, dances, visual arts, and theater.
    • Chocolate Hills Most Popular in Bohol
      Spread over 50 kilometers. Hundreds of green limestone hills covering the landscape. Named "Chocolate Hills" because grass turns brown during dry season. Can be viewed by land (climbing the highest point) or by air (air tours).
    • Loboc River
      Features a floating restaurant. Visitors enjoy a river cruise with lunch, serenaded by local musicians and entertained by local performers.
    • Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary (Corella, Bohol)
      Ecotourism destination by the Philippine Tarsier Foundation. Protects Tarsiers (mamag) — tiny endangered primates that fit in the palm of a hand.
    • Panglao, Cabilao & Balicasag Islands (Anda town)
      Explore underwater marine life including unique corals and nudibranchs.
    Cebu "Queen City of the South"
    Cebu City = oldest city in the Philippines. Filled with Spanish colonial relics from the 16th century. Considered birthplace of Christianity in the Far East.
    • Magellan's Cross
      First planted in 1521 under Ferdinand Magellan — marked the birth of Christianity in the Philippines and the start of 300 years of Spanish colonization. People believed the original cross had miraculous healing powers and chipped it away; it was later encased in another wooden cross.
    • Santo Niño Basilica
      Home to the original and oldest religious relic of the world-famous Infant Jesus. Sinulog Festival — the city's well-known annual cultural and religious festival — is held to honor this relic.
    • Oslob (Whale Shark Watching)
      Where people can swim with whale sharks, the largest fish in the world. Has led to stronger efforts to protect the marine ecosystem.
    • Cebu Safari and Adventure Park
      Premier safari park in northern Cebu. Sanctuary for hundreds of plants and animals. Main features: African Savanna, Michelle's Garden, Gardens of the World, and the White Lion Safari.
    📍 Also in Region VII: Cabagnow Cave Pool (Bohol) · Bantayan Island · Colon Street (Cebu)
    Region VIII Eastern Visayas ▾
    Eastern Samar
    • Homonhon Island Historic Landmark
      Where Ferdinand Magellan and his men first set foot on Philippine soil on March 16, 1521.
    • Ando Island & Divinubo Island
      Ando = quaint island with colorful floral gardens. Divinubo = "Garden Island" facing the Pacific Ocean — white sand beach, coral reefs, rock formations, mangroves.
    Leyte
    • San Juanico Bridge
      Crosses the San Juanico Strait, bridging the islands of Samar and Leyte.
    • Kalanggaman Island
      Name comes from 'langgam' (bird in Bisaya). When viewed from above, the island's two ends look like bird's wings flapping with the changing tides.
    📍 Also in Region VIII: Sambawan Island (Biliran) · Cambaro Cave (Southern Leyte) · Sila Island Pink Beach (Northern Samar) · Sohoton Caves & Natural Bridge (Samar)
    Mindanao
    Region IX Zamboanga Peninsula ▾
    Zamboanga del Norte Capital: Dipolog
    • The Rizal Shrine Park (Dapitan City)
      Major historical landmark where Dr. Jose Rizal once lived. He decorated the central plaza with gumamela (hibiscus) and acacia trees, helped create a relief map of Mindanao, and built a house for his family on 16 hectares of land (after winning a small lottery).
    • P'gsalabuk Circle (Dipolog)
      Resin-made figure of 3 people symbolizing Dipolog's three diverse cultures: Lumads, Muslims, and Christians. They hold a bowl raised to the heavens as a gesture of thanksgiving; pouring water signifies God's blessing on the tri-people.
    📍 Also in Region IX: Gloria's Fantasyland (Zamboanga del Norte) · Pulacan Falls (Zamboanga del Sur) · Rotunda Obelisk (Zamboanga Sibugay)
    Region X Northern Mindanao ▾
    Bukidnon 3rd Largest Province in PH
    Ecotourism and heritage destination — adventure, nature, cultural, traditional, and religious experiences.
    • Mount Capistrano
      793-meter mountain with a fantastic panoramic view of Central Mindanao.
    • Dahilayan Forest Park Resort
      Located at the foot of Mt. Kitanglad. Fun activities: go-cart, ATV, bumper boat, mini golf.
    • Abbey of the Transfiguration
      Pyramidal church and Benedictine monastery in Malaybalay City.
    • Kaamulan Festival
      Ethnic and cultural festival featuring cultural performers, local agricultural produce, and a rodeo. Held every February or March in Malaybalay City.
    Camiguin "Island Born of Fire"
    Known as "Island Born of Fire" because of 7 volcanoes shaping its landscape. Abundant natural beauty: hot & cold springs, waterfalls, lakes, white-sand beaches, dive sites.
    • Mt. Timpoong & Mt. Hibok-Hibok Range Natural Monument ASEAN Heritage Park
      Encompasses Mt. Hibok-Hibok (active volcano) and Mt. Timpoong. Declared an ASEAN Heritage Park in 2015 for its wide range of endemic flora and fauna.
    • Sunken Cemetery
      Resting place of thousands who died during the eruption of the Old Vulcan. Today visitors can dive and snorkel over the submerged cemetery.
    Misamis Oriental Capital: Cagayan de Oro
    Cagayan de Oro = "City of Golden Friendship". Mindanao's business, trade, IT, and adventure tourism center.
    • Cagayan de Oro River Rapids
      White water rafting through 90 kilometers of rapids. PH's best whitewater rafting experience.
    • Gardens of Malasag Mountain
      7.2-hectare cultural village in a botanical garden setting showcasing different cultures of Northern Mindanao.
    📍 Also in Region X: Maria Cristina Falls (Lanao del Norte) · Baliangao Wetland Park & Dolphin Island (Misamis Occidental)
    Region XI Davao Region ▾
    Davao del Sur / Davao City
    Home to Mt. Apo — highest mountain in the Philippines. Davao del Norte = "Durian and Banana Capital of the Philippines."
    • People's Park
      4-hectare cultural-theme park with mini-forest, man-made falls, dancing fountain, fishponds, children's playground, and a giant durian dome. One of the cleanest and greenest parks in the Philippines — dubbed best park in PH.
    • Mount Apo (Apo Sandawa) Highest Mountain in PH
      Highest mountain in the Philippines and potentially active volcano. Considered the grandfather of Philippine mountains — sacred in local mythology. Significant climb for local mountaineers.
    • Philippine Eagle Center
      Home to the endangered Philippine eagle. Houses several birds of prey at the foothills of Mt. Apo.
    📍 Also in Region XI: Malagos Garden Resort (Davao del Sur) · Samal Island (Davao del Norte) · Mt. Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary — UNESCO Heritage Site (Davao Oriental)
    Region XII SOCCSKSARGEN ▾
    Cotabato "Fruit Basket of Mindanao"
    Agriculture-based industry. Rio Grande de Mindanao = 2nd largest river in the Philippines — located here.
    • Lake Agco ("Garden of Eden" of Kidapawan)
      Natural Spa Village — a steaming hot lake where the Manobo tribe applies sulfuric mud spa on visitors, along with natural steam and hot bath.
    • Asik-Asik Falls
      Unique 140-meter curtain-like spring falls popular among nature lovers.
    South Cotabato "Land of the Dreamweavers"
    Home of the T'boli Tribe — distinguished by colorful clothes, bracelets, earrings, complicated beadwork, woven fabric, and beautiful brass ornaments.
    • Lake Sebu
      7 magnificent falls interconnected and viewable through an aerial float (zipline).
    • Bakngeb River Cave
      Wide river pool at its outlet for swimming. Underground cave system makes water naturally cold and crystal clear.
    📍 Also in Region XII: Marguez Hot & Cold Spring (Sultan Kudarat) · Gumasa Beach (Sarangani) · Sanchez Peak (General Santos City)
    Region XIII Caraga ▾
    Surigao del Norte
    Two major islands: Siargao Island & Bucas Grande Island. Siargao = teardrop-shaped island — the Surfing Capital of the Philippines. Home to some of the best beaches in Asia.
    • Cloud 9 Beach (Siargao)
      Main surfing spot. Venue of the Siargao International Surfing Cup held annually. Features a boardwalk leading to a 3-story viewing platform for watching surfers and catching sunrise/sunset.
    • Magpupungko Rock Pools
      Natural rock pools that reveal themselves during low tide. Jewel-toned saltwater pools scattered across the landscape — deep enough for cliff diving; great for swimming and snorkeling.
    • Tayangban Cave Pool
      Cave with beautiful stalactite formations. Navigated using ropes and bamboo. Features emerald green cenotes and cliff-jumping spots.
    • Naked Island, Daku Island & Guyam Island
      Available for single-day island hopping activities.
    Surigao del Sur
    Located on the northeastern coast of Mindanao, facing the Pacific Ocean. Diwata Mountain Range lines its northwestern boundary.
    • Tinuy-an Falls "Niagara Falls of PH"
      3-tiered land formation creating pools of varying sizes and elevation. Known as the "Niagara Falls of the Philippines."
    • Enchanted River
      Turquoise blue lagoon with crystal clear water — looks like a natural aquarium with colorful fish of different species and sizes.
    📍 Also in Region XIII: Balangay Shrine Museum (Agusan del Norte) · Agusan Marsh Wildlife Sanctuary (Agusan del Sur) · PBMA Shrine (Dinagat Islands)
    BARMM Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao ▾
    Maguindanao
    Numerous natural attractions full of potential, waiting to be rediscovered.
    • Masjid Dimaukom (Pink Mosque of Maguindanao)
      Situated in Datu Saudi Ampatuan town. Completed in mid-2014, financed by Mayor Samsodin Dimaukom. Pink color symbolizes peace and love (also his wife's favorite color). Notably, Christian workers built this Islamic structure to emphasize unity and faith brotherhood.
    • Blue Lagoon
      Has naturally bluish water rare to be found anywhere else.
    📍 Also in BARMM: Malamawi Beach & Sta. Isabel Cathedral (Basilan) · Panampangan Island (Tawi-Tawi) · Mount Ragang (Lanao del Sur)
