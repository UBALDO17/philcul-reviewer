NCR
Manila

Philippines' capital; top shopping, arts, cultural immersion. Near Laguna de Bai lakeshore towns.

  • Intramuros
    Oldest historic place in Manila; contains San Agustin Church (UNESCO) & Fort Santiago (built 1571).
  • Rizal Park
    People's park with sculptures, gardens, event venues; flagship of freedom.
  • National Museum of the Philippines
    Houses Fine Arts, Anthropology, Natural History museums & National Planetarium.
  • Manila Ocean Park
    Country's first world-class marine theme park; features the Oceanarium.

🎨
NCR
Quezon City

Most populous city in Metro Manila; tourism focused on cultural, historical, educational activities.

  • Quezon Memorial Circle
    27-hectare national park dedicated to President Manuel L. Quezon; houses a museum & his remains.
  • Art in Island Museum
    3D art museum founded by Yun Jae Kyoung with 18 Korean master painters; immersive photo backdrops.
HistoryCulture
🌲
CAR
Benguet / Baguio

"Salad Bowl of the Philippines." Baguio = "Summer Capital" & "City of Pines" (avg 15–23°C).

  • Baguio Cathedral
    Distinct pink facade, rose windows & twin belfry (completed 1936).
  • Burnham Park
    Premier oldest park; gardens, lake, biking, skating, boat rides.
  • Wright Park & The Mansion
    Horseback riding, souvenirs; The Mansion = official summer residence of the Philippine president.
  • Colors of Stobosa
    Hillside home artwork at La Trinidad; symbolizes reblooming & re-greening of the environment.
NatureCulture
🌾
CAR
Ifugao

Known for the bulul (rice deity figure); festival: Gotad Ad Ifugao (June).

  • Banaue Rice Terraces
    "Eighth Wonder of the World" & UNESCO World Heritage Site; 2,000-year-old cascading rice terraces by Ifugao ancestors.
  • Philippine War Memorial Shrine
    Commemorates WWII's end; pyramid-type structure in Kiangan depicting the defeat of Gen. Yamashita.
UNESCOHistory
🪡
CAR
Kalinga

Home of the last and oldest mambababatok (traditional tattoo artist).

  • Apo Whang-Od
    Located in Buscalan Village; over 70 years of tattooing Kalinga warriors. Uses calamansi thorn, soot & water pigment as traditional tools.
Culture

Region I
Ilocos Region

Ilocos Norte capital: Laoag (northernmost urban hub). Ilocos Sur: Vigan City — most well-preserved Spanish colonial town (UNESCO World Heritage).

  • Paoay Church
    UNESCO World Heritage; Saint Augustine Church; baroque/Spanish colonial architecture.
  • La Paz Sand Dunes
    Sandy coastal desert near Laoag; sand surfing & ATV rides.
  • Calle Crisologo
    Cobblestone street in Vigan with Spanish-era houses & horse-drawn carriages (calesas).
  • Bantay Bell Tower
    Built 1591 as a watchtower; iconic symbol of Vigan.
UNESCOHeritage
🌊
Region II
Cagayan Valley

Batanes = northernmost & smallest province; indigenous people = Ivatans.

  • Honesty Coffee Shop (Batanes)
    Unmanned refreshment store worldwide fame — buyers pay on honor system in a wooden box.
  • Callao Cave (Cagayan)
    7-chambered cave with massive limestones, 7 skylights, and a chapel; contains oldest human remains in PH.
  • Palaui Island
    7,000-hectare protected ecotourism sanctuary; pristine ecosystems & geological formations.
NatureHistory
✝️
Region III
Central Luzon

"Rice Granary of the Philippines." Clark International Airport located here (Pampanga).

  • Las Casas Filipinas De Acuzar (Bataan)
    Beach resort, convention center & heritage destination with restored heritage houses.
  • Mt. Samat Shrine / Dambana ng Kagitingan
    72-hectare memorial honoring Filipino-American forces against Japan in WWII. Features a Colonnade & Memorial Cross.
HistoryNature

Region IV-A
CALABARZON

"Industrial Powerhouse of the Philippines." South & east of Metro Manila.

  • Taal Volcano (Batangas)
    Smallest active volcano in the Philippines; accessible by boat then trek/horseback.
  • Pagsanjan Falls (Laguna)
    "Shooting the rapids" boat ride by accredited boatmen through Magdapio River.
  • Enchanted Kingdom (Laguna)
    First and only world-class theme park in the Philippines.
  • Angono-Binangonan Petroglyphs (Rizal)
    Oldest Filipino work of art; discovered 1965 by Carlos "Botong" Francisco; 127 stone-age rock engravings.
NatureHistory

Region IV-B
Palawan

Largest province in the Philippines (~1,780 islands). Houses 2 UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

  • Puerto Princesa Subterranean River
    UNESCO World Heritage; limestone karst landscape; underground river emptying directly into the sea.
  • Tubbataha Reef Marine Park
    UNESCO; 96,828 hectares in Sulu Sea; North Islet is nesting site for marine turtles & birds; 100-m perpendicular wall.
  • Calauit Safari Park
    3,700-hectare wildlife reserve with critically endangered species native to Palawan's Calamian island chain.
UNESCONatureBeach

Region V
Bicol – Albay

Also known as Bicolandia; southernmost tip of Luzon.

  • Mayon Volcano National Park
    Most active volcano in the Philippines; perfect cone shape; home to threatened & endangered species. ATV tours available.
  • Cagsawa Ruins
    Remains of a 16th-century Franciscan church destroyed by Mayon's 1814 eruption; only belfry survived.
NatureHistory
🏖️
Region VI
Boracay, Aklan

"Beach Capital of the Philippines." Ranked #1 in Condé Nast Traveler's Top Island (Asia) 2022. Aklan = oldest province in the country.

  • Willy's Rock
    Popular attraction with a Virgin Mary statue atop man-made staircase on a rock.
  • Boracay Oceanarium
    Longest underwater tunnel in the Philippines; houses one of the world's largest pearls.
  • Ati-Atihan Festival
    Held every January in Kalibo; Mardi Gras-style; one of the most popular festivals in the country.
BeachCulture
🎭
NIR
Negros Island Region

Bacolod = "City of Smiles"; rich culinary heritage. Dumaguete = "City of Gentle People."

  • The Ruins (Negros Occidental)
    "Taj Mahal of Negros Occidental." Remnant mansion of sugar baron Don Mariano Lacson; burned in WWII.
  • Apo Island (Negros Oriental)
    One of the best diving spots; swim with pawikans (sea turtles) & rich marine biodiversity.
  • Siquijor
    Formerly "Isla de Fuegos" (Island of Fire) — fireflies on molave trees mistaken for fire by Spaniards. Southernmost Visayan island.
BeachHistory
🐠
Region VII
Central Visayas

Cebu City = oldest city in PH, birthplace of Christianity in Far East. "Queen City of the South."

  • Chocolate Hills (Bohol)
    50 km spread; hundreds of conical limestone hills that turn brown (chocolate) in dry season. Unique geological formation.
  • Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary (Bohol)
    Ecotourism site in Corella protecting the endangered tarsier (mamag); established by Philippine Tarsier Foundation.
  • Magellan's Cross (Cebu)
    First planted in 1521 by Ferdinand Magellan; marks the birth of Christianity in the Philippines. Encased in wooden cross to preserve it.
  • Oslob Whale Shark Interaction
    Swim with the largest fish in the world; strengthened marine ecosystem protection efforts.
NatureHistoryBeach
🌉
Region VIII
Eastern Visayas

Homonhon Island: where Ferdinand Magellan first set foot on Philippine soil (March 16, 1521).

  • San Juanico Bridge (Leyte)
    Crosses San Juanico Strait connecting Samar & Leyte islands.
  • Kalanggaman Island (Leyte)
    Name means "bird" in Bisaya; island resembles flapping bird wings when viewed from above.
HistoryBeach
🏛️
Region IX
Zamboanga Peninsula

Dapitan City rich in Jose Rizal history; P'gsalabuk = "Orchid City" (Dipolog).

  • Rizal Shrine Park (Dapitan)
    Dr. Rizal decorated the plaza & created a relief map of Mindanao; built a house for his family on 16 hectares — now a major landmark.
  • P'gsalabuk Circle (Dipolog)
    Resin figure of 3 people representing Lumads, Muslims & Christians — symbolizing unity holding a bowl to the heavens in thanksgiving.
HistoryCulture
🌿
Region X
Northern Mindanao

Camiguin = "Island Born of Fire" (7 volcanoes). Cagayan de Oro = "City of Golden Friendship."

  • Mt. Hibok-Hibok Range Natural Monument
    Declared ASEAN Heritage Park (2015); encompasses active Mt. Hibok-Hibok & Mt. Timpoong in Camiguin.
  • Sunken Cemetery (Camiguin)
    Resting place of thousands who died in eruption of Old Vulcan; tourists can dive and snorkel here.
  • Cagayan de Oro River Rapids
    90 km of white water rafting; adventure tourism center of Mindanao.
NatureAdventure
🦅
Region XI
Davao Region

"Fruit Basket of the Philippines." Home to Mt. Apo, the highest mountain in the country.

  • Mount Apo
    Highest mountain in the Philippines (locally: Apo Sandawa); potentially active volcano; sacred in local mythology.
  • Philippine Eagle Center
    Home to the endangered Philippine eagle and birds of prey in Mt. Apo's foothills.
  • People's Park
    4-hectare cultural-theme park with mini-forest, man-made falls, dancing fountain & giant durian dome. Best park in PH.
NatureCulture
💧
Region XII
SOCCSKSARGEN

Cotabato = "Fruit Basket of Mindanao." South Cotabato = "Land of the Dreamweavers" (T'boli Tribe).

  • Lake Agco (Cotabato)
    "Garden of Eden" of Kidapawan City; steaming hot lake used for sulfuric mud spa by Manobos tribe.
  • Lake Sebu (South Cotabato)
    Home of the T'boli Tribe; 7 magnificent interconnected falls viewable via aerial float.
  • Asik-Asik Falls
    Unique 140-meter curtain-like spring falls; popular among nature lovers.
NatureCulture
🏄
Region XIII
Caraga

Siargao Island = "Surfing Capital of the Philippines." Teardrop-shaped island.

  • Cloud 9 Beach (Siargao)
    Main surf spot; hosts the annual Siargao International Surfing Cup; 3-story viewing platform.
  • Magpupungko Rock Pools
    Natural saltwater pools revealed during low tide; great for diving & snorkeling from low cliffs.
  • Tinuy-an Falls (Surigao del Sur)
    "Niagara Falls of the Philippines" — 3 tiers forming pools of varying sizes and elevation.
  • Enchanted River (Surigao del Sur)
    Turquoise blue lagoon with crystal clear water; natural aquarium with colorful fish.
BeachNature
🕌
BARMM
Bangsamoro Region

Formerly ARMM; renamed after 2019 plebiscite. Muslim-dominant autonomous region.

  • Masjid Dimaukom (Pink Mosque)
    Situated in Datu Saudi Ampatuan; completed mid-2014; pink color symbolizes peace & love; built by Christian workers to emphasize unity.
  • Blue Lagoon (Maguindanao)
    Naturally bluish water rare to be found anywhere else in the world.
CultureNature
